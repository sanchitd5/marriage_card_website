Note: the source model shipped as a static (unrigged, unanimated) 7-mesh
character set. Rigged in Blender (headless, scripted) with a custom
13-bone biped armature in a hanging-arms bind pose, matching the joint set
the site's procedural choreography engine drives (see js/app/kinetic-dancer.js).

Re-rigged from a pristine, never-round-tripped export
(source/untitled.gltf from the fuller Sketchfab download package) after
the first rigging pass -- built from a single .glb export and repeatedly
patched in place -- accumulated damage from a known Blender bug where
re-importing an already-exported skinned glTF explodes it into hundreds
of mesh objects. This rebuild applies object transforms (the classic
"unapplied 0.01 scale" cause of a 100x rest-pose blowup) before parenting,
uses Blender's automatic (heat) weights where the solver succeeds, and a
rigid nearest-bone-segment fallback (single-bone weight, no blending) for
meshes the heat solver cannot traverse -- the Face and Hairs meshes, both
built from many disconnected geometry islands (a face plate and hundreds
of separate hair-strand pieces) that heat diffusion cannot solve across.
The Chest mesh (the wing/pauldron piece) has its spurious blend weight
onto the arm bones removed and renormalized, so it moves with the torso
rather than tearing at the shoulder seam when an arm swings through a
real choreography angle.

Textures: baseColor images restored from the same pristine download
package's named texture files (BaseColor Chest/Face/Legs/Lower/Shirt/Band),
downscaled and JPEG-compressed. The runtime renders the dancer as a
chrome MatCap pass with the real texture multiplied in, plus a thin
wireframe accent pass on top -- see js/app/kinetic-dancer.js.
